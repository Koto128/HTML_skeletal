HISTORY

Early History

The British brought cricket to India in the early 1700s, with the first cricket match played in 1721.[15] In 1848, the Parsi community in Mumbai formed the Oriental Cricket Club, the first cricket club to be established by Indians. After slow beginnings, the Europeans eventually invited the Parsis to play a match in 1877.[16] By 1912, the Parsis, Hindus, Sikhs and Muslims of Bombay played a quadrangular tournament with the Europeans every year.[16] In the early 1900s, some Indians went on to play for the England cricket team. Some of these, such as Ranjitsinhji and Duleepsinhji were greatly appreciated by the British and their names went on to be used for the Ranji Trophy and Duleep Trophy â€“ two major first-class tournaments in India. In 1911, an Indian men's cricket team, captained by Bhupinder Singh of Patiala, went on their first official tour of the British Isles, but only played English county teams and not the England cricket team.

21st century

The Indian team underwent major improvements under the captaincy of Sourav Ganguly and guidance of John Wright, India's first foreign coach.[49][50] In the Kolkata Test match, India became only the third team in the history of Test cricket to win a Test match after following on. Australian captain Steve Waugh labelled India as the "Final Frontier" because of his side's inability to win a Test series in India.[51] In the year 2002, India were joint-winners of the ICC Champions Trophy with Sri Lanka and then went to the 2003 Cricket World Cup in South Africa, where they reached the final, only to be beaten by Australia. A convincing ODI series win in Pakistan in early 2006, following a loss in the Test series, gave India the world record of 17 successive ODI victories while batting second.In September 2007, India won the first-ever ICC Men's T20 World Cup held in South Africa, beating Pakistan by 5 runs in the final.[53] On 2 April 2011, India won the 2011 CricketWorld Cup by defeating Sri Lanka in the final, thus becoming the third team after West Indies and Australia to win the World Cup twice.[54] India also became the first team to win the World Cup on home soil.[55] India defeated England in the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy Final and captain M. S. Dhoni became the first men's cricket team captain in history to win the three major ICC trophies, namely the Cricket World Cup, ICC Men's T20 World Cup and ICC Champions Trophy. [56][57]In the 2014 ICC Men's World Twenty20 hosted in Bangladesh, India narrowly missed out on another ICC trophy by losing to Sri Lanka in the final.[58] India was knocked out of the 2015 Cricket World Cup in the semi-final to eventual winners Australia.[59] India then began 2016 by winning the 2016 Asia Cup, remaining unbeaten throughout the tournament.[60] The team were favourites to win the 2016 ICC World Twenty20, which was being held at home, but lost in the semi-final to eventual champions West Indies.[61] India defeatedPakistan in their first game of the 2017 ICC Champions Trophy but lost to the same opponents in the final, the first time they had met at this stage of a tournament since 2007.[62][63]

Team Colours

India plays its test cricket matches with the traditional cricket whites with the caps and helmets being navy blue, while the uniforms worn in limited-overs matches have different shades of blue for ODIs and T20s, which sometimes has a splash of the colours which are present in Indian flag.[80]During the 1992 and 1999 Cricket World Cups, the Indian team's kit was sponsored by ISC and ASICS respectively,[81][82] but had been without an official kit sponsor till 2001. With no official kit sponsor for the Indian team, Omtex manufactured the shirts and pants for the team, while some players chose to wear pants provided to them by their individual sponsors like Adidas and Reebok till December 2005.In December 2005, Nike outbid its competitors Adidas and Reebok, and acquired the contract for 5 years which started in January 2006 ahead of Indian team's tour to Pakistan.[83] Nike was a long time kit supplier to team India with two extensions for a period of five years each time; in 2011[84] and 2016[85] respectively.After Nike ended its contract in September 2020,[86] MPL Sports Apparel & Accessories, a subsidiary of online gaming platform Mobile Premier League replaced Nike as the kit manufacturer in November 2020 ahead of Indian team's tour to Australia, which was supposed to run until December 2023.[87][88]In November 2022, MPL Sports decided to exit the deal before the end of their contract and hand over their rights to Kewal Kiran Clothing Limited (KKCL).[89] In January 2023, MPL appointed Kewal Kiran Clothing Limited (KKCL) and Killer Jeans (a brand owned by KKCL) as interim sponsors till May 2023.[90][91] In February 2023, it was announced that Adidas will begin a five year sponsorship deal in June 2023 ahead of ICC World Test Championship final, replacing KKCL.[92] In May 2023, BCCI officially announced Adidas as their kit sponsor for next five years running until March 2028.

Captains

The Indian cricket team has had several captains throughout its history, each contributing to the team's success and leaving their mark on Indian cricket. Here is a list of some of the notable captains of the Indian cricket team:

  1. C.K. Nayudu: He was India's first Test captain and led the team in its debut Test series against England in 1932.
  2. Vijay Hazare: Hazare was India's captain in the post-independence era and led the team during the 1950s. He was known for his disciplined approach.
  3. Vinoo Mankad: A versatile cricketer, Mankad served as India's captain in the 1950s and was known for his all-round abilities.
  4. Polly Umrigar: Umrigar was a prominent figure in Indian cricket during the 1950s and 1960s and led the team in both Test and ODI cricket.
  5. Mansoor Ali Khan Pataudi: Fondly known as "Tiger," Pataudi was a charismatic leader who captained India from 1962 to 1975. He played a crucial role in shaping Indian cricket during his tenure.
  6. Ajit Wadekar: Wadekar was India's captain during a successful period in the early 1970s when India registered its first-ever Test series victories in England and the West Indies.
  7. Sunil Gavaskar: The legendary batsman Gavaskar captained India for a brief period, including during the 1979 Cricket World Cup.
  8. Kapil Dev: Kapil Dev led India to its first-ever Cricket World Cup victory in 1983. He was a dynamic all-rounder and a charismatic leader.
  9. Ravi Shastri: Shastri served as captain for a brief period in the late 1980s.
  10. Mohammad Azharuddin: Azharuddin was a stylish batsman and captained India in both Test and ODI cricket from the late 1980s to the late 1990s.
  11. Sachin Tendulkar: Tendulkar, one of the greatest batsmen of all time, had a brief stint as captain of the Indian team.
  12. Sourav Ganguly: Ganguly, also known as "Dada," played a pivotal role in changing the team's attitude and competitiveness during his tenure as captain from 2000 to 2005.
  13. Rahul Dravid: Dravid, a true gentleman of the game, captained India in both Test and ODI formats and was known for his astute cricketing mind.
  14. Anil Kumble: Kumble, India's leading wicket-taker in Test cricket, served as captain for a brief period in 2007-08.
  15. MS Dhoni: Dhoni is one of the most successful captains in Indian cricket history. He led India to victory in the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, and the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup.
  16. Virat Kohli: Kohli took over the captaincy from Dhoni and has been the captain in all formats of the game. Under his leadership, the Indian cricket team achieved numerous milestones.

These captains have played a significant role in shaping the Indian cricket team's history and have left a lasting legacy in the world of cricket. Each of them brought their unique leadership style and cricketing expertise to the team, contributing to the team's growth and success on the international stage.

Fan Following

Cricket in India has an immense and passionate fan following that transcends age, background, and region. Matches involving the Indian cricket team, especially those against rivals like Pakistan, are watched by millions and often break television rating records. Indian cricket stadiums witness large and enthusiastic crowds, creating electric atmospheres during matches. Iconic players like Sachin Tendulkar, Virat Kohli, and Kapil Dev are celebrated as cultural icons. Street cricket is a common sight in Indian neighborhoods, reflecting the sport's popularity at the grassroots level. The Indian Premier League (IPL) is a major cricketing event with a massive fan base. Social media is abuzz with cricket discussions, and cricket merchandise sales are robust. Cricket evokes strong emotions, both in victory and defeat, and serves as a unifying force in a diverse nation. Overall, cricket is deeply ingrained in the fabric of Indian society and culture, and its popularity shows no signs of diminishing.

Conclusionz

The Indian cricket team's journey from its inception to its current stature as a cricketing powerhouse is a testament to dedication, talent, and unwavering support. With a rich history, iconic players, and a passionate fan base, the team continues to inspire generations and holds the promise of achieving even greater heights in the future. The Indian cricket team is not just a sporting entity; it is a source of national pride and unity, symbolizing the spirit of excellence and resilience.
